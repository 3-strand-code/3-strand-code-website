FAQ

How does it work?

You will learn everything you need to execute production projects, start to finish. This course will give you the knowledge and skills necessary to enter the job market as a web developer. Expect to be able to build a custom website from scratch and get it online. We’ll cover topics such as:

  • Environment & Terminal
  • Git & GitHub
  • Editors & IDEs
  • Package Managers
  • HTML5 & CSS3
  • Preprocessors
  • Front end Frameworks
  • Intro to JavaScript & jQuery
  • Builds & Deployment
  • Hosting & Maintenance
  • Google Analytics
  • Backends As A Service

What is the schedule?

The classes begin on x and go for one month. The classes will be held two times per week. Each class period is two hours long from 5pm to 7pm, Tuesday and Thursday nights.

Where do we meet?

IC:Work/CoWork at 410 Sherman Ave Coeur d'Alene, Idaho 83814

What will I work on ?

Anything you want. This course is open to the ideas of the students and instructors. The initial step will be presenting ideas and collectively we will vote on the project to build.

Can I work on my own project?

Absolutely as long as it fits into the scope of the class.

Will I work solo or in a group?

You can work solo, but it’s highly recommended to participate as a group.

Will the class times be hands on or lecture?

Class time will be a mixture of both, but mostly focused on hands on creating and mentoring.

What do I need to bring?

  • A laptop or computer
  • Project ideas
  • Insatiable curiosity.

Will there be homework?

There won’t be homework in the traditional sense, but students are highly encouraged to work on the projects outside of class time.

What level do I have to be at?

Students should have a strong understanding of computers and technology, and have a basic understanding of coding or design. This course is intended for those with strong interest and some level of skills. Basic knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript is suggested. No previous coding experience is required, however, those familiar with HTML, CSS or JavaScript will benefit most.
