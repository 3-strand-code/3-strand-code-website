FAQ

What will I learn?

You will learn every step to execute production projects, start to finish. This course will give you the knowledge and skills necessary to enter the job market as a web developer. Expect to be able to build a custom website from scratch and get it online. We’ll cover topics such as:

  • Environment and terminal
  • Git and GitHub
  • Editors and IDEs
  • Package managers
  • HTML5 and CSS3
  • Preprocessors
  • Front end Frameworks
  • Intro to JavaScript & jQuery
  • Builds and deployment
  • Hosting and maintenance
  • Google Analytics
  • Backend as a Service

What is the schedule?

The classes begin on June 1st and go for eight weeks. The classes will be on Monday and Thursday evenings from 6pm to 8pm.

Where do we meet?

IC:Work/CoWork at 410 Sherman Ave Coeur d'Alene, Idaho 83814

Will the classes be hands on or lecture?

Classes will be a mixture of both. The first part of the class will consist of an introduction to the topic and the remaining time will be spent practicing and building with the knowledge gained.

What will I work on?

Anything you want. This course is open to the ideas of the students and instructors. The initial step will be presenting ideas and collectively we will vote on the projects to build.

Can I work on my own project?

Absolutely as long as it fits into the scope of the class.

Will I work solo or in a group?

You can work solo, but it’s highly recommended to participate as a group.

What do I need to bring?

  • A laptop or computer
  • Project ideas
  • Insatiable curiosity.

Will there be homework?

There won’t be homework in the traditional sense, but students are encouraged to work on the projects outside of class time.

What skill level do I have to be at?

This course is intended for those with strong interest and some level of skills. Basic knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript is suggested. No previous coding experience is required, however, those familiar with HTML, CSS or JavaScript will benefit most.
